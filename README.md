# NetHack AI Research Playground

Experimental research repository focused on autonomous agents for NetHack.

NetHack has become a major benchmark environment for reinforcement learning and long-horizon reasoning research through projects such as:
- NLE (NetHack Learning Environment)
- MiniHack
- hierarchical RL research
- open-ended RL environments

Research organizations including Meta AI have used NetHack-based environments to study:
- exploration
- planning
- skill acquisition
- memory
- language-conditioned agents
- hierarchical reinforcement learning

This repository explores similar ideas from an engineering-heavy perspective, combining:
- symbolic systems
- RL agents
- scripted skills
- human strategies
- LLM-assisted tooling
- autonomous experimentation loops

The project is also heavily inspired by decades of accumulated NetHack player knowledge and tactics.  
Some experiments intentionally reuse strategies discovered by human players over the last 30+ years, while others attempt to discover entirely new behaviors through autonomous training and experimentation.

Beyond simply trying to "finish the game", this repository also explores:
- modified NetHack environments
- custom game mechanics
- agent evaluation environments
- multi-agent experimentation
- autonomous AI research workflows
- replay analysis and visualization
- tooling around NLE and NetHack ecosystems

The long-term goal is to explore what kinds of architectures, training methods and hybrid systems are actually capable of handling the extreme complexity of NetHack and similar open-ended environments.
